Tteokbokki

Description

Tteokbokki, also spelled dukbokki or topokki, is one of South Korea’s most beloved street foods and a quintessential dish in Korean cuisine. For those who never tried Tteokbokki, it’s a spicy, saucy dish made of Korean rice cakes and various toppings. The texture of the rice cake is chewy and bouncy, and super satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 onion sliced (optional)
  • 1/4 cabbage sliced
  • 2 green onion -- 2-inch pieces
  • 3 cups frozen rice cake
  • 2 cups of dashi stock or vegetable stock
  • 1.5-2 tbsp of gochujang
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 0.5 tbsp of sugar
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 3 tsp of gochugaru (korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 cup of mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup fish cakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Turn the heat up to medium-high and add Gochujang, Sugar, Gochugaru, Honey, Garlic, black pepper, and Soy Sauce.
  2. Add a splash of dashi stock, Mix, and let it simmer for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Pour in the rest of the dashi stock and bring to a simmer. Once simmering, add in onion and let it simmer for 3 minutes. (optional)
  4. Add rice cake and cook for another 5-6 minutes; make sure that all the rice cake is soaked in the sauce.
  5. Finally, add in any toppings of your choice, I like fish cake and cabbage, and simmer for another 3-4 minutes.
  6. Optional but highly recommend: add 1 cup of cheese and broil it in the oven till cheese is melted, or cover with a lid on so the cheese fully melts.
